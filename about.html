Our Story

Falling Feathers Hollow is a family-built sanctuary-style backyard farm where animals are cared for, people are reminded to breathe, and purpose grows through everyday work.

How the Hollow Started

It started with two ducks, a little piece of hope, and the question: what if this could become more than a backyard?

From there, Falling Feathers Hollow began growing into a place of second chances. Ducks turned into chickens. Chickens turned into quail. Everyday chores turned into something meaningful. The hollow is still being built, one fence post, one coop improvement, one rescued animal, and one honest day of work at a time.

We are not trying to be a factory farm. We are building a place where the animals come first, the eggs are part of the story, and the community can see that care, creativity, and faith still matter.

Today, that means caring for ducks, chickens, quail, and rescues while offering local Chicken, Duck, & Quail Eggs when supply allows. It also means creating a peaceful space tucked into the woods and creek life of West Virginia, where kids can learn and families can connect.
