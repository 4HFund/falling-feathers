Meet Our Flock

Falling Feathers Hollow is a small sanctuary-style backyard farm tucked into the hollow, surrounded by woods, creek sounds, and a growing crew of ducks, chickens, quail, and rescues.

Animal sanctuary Chicken, Duck, & Quail Eggs Family built Second chances

The Feathered Family

Some are loud. Some are fancy. Some act like they own the place. Every one of them has a story and a reason they matter here.

Ducks

Waddles & Ming Ming

The original duck duo. They helped start the whole dream and still bring the perfect mix of chaos, comedy, and muddy footprints.

Chicken Crew

Yolkie & the Girls

Yolkie, Little Foot, Pepper, Patty, Popsicle, and McChicken bring personality to the nest and help make the chicken egg side of the hollow possible.

Rescues

Pip, Puddles, Moon Beam & Friends

Our flock keeps growing as animals find their way here. The goal is simple: safe space, good care, and a place where overlooked animals are loved.

Chicken, Duck, & Quail Eggs

When the flock is laying and supply allows, we offer local eggs with the same care we give the animals themselves.

Duck Eggs

Tiny Powerhouse

Quail Eggs

Small, speckled eggs that are fun, snackable, and popular with people who want something unique for meals or pet toppers.

For Families and Four-Legged Friends

Some customers ask about eggs for their dogs, especially quail eggs. We keep that simple and honest.

Quail Eggs for Dogs

Many pet owners use quail eggs as an occasional whole-food topper or special treat. Start small, keep it balanced, and check with your vet if your dog has allergies or health concerns.

Local and Seasonal

Egg availability changes with the flock, the weather, and the season. We would rather be honest about supply than overpromise.

Care Comes First

This is not a factory setup. The animals come first, and the eggs are part of a bigger mission of stewardship, rescue, and community.

Built feather by feather

Falling Feathers Hollow is becoming a place where animals are safe, kids can learn, families can connect, and everyday work turns into purpose.
